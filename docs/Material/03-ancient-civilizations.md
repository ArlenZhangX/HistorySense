# Ancient Civilizations

**Time Range:** c. 3500 BCE -- 800 BCE

## Background

For most of human history, there were no cities, kingdoms, or written
records.

People lived in small groups as hunters and gatherers. They moved from
place to place, following animals, seasons, and available plants.

Humans had language, tools, and culture long before civilization
emerged. What they lacked was stability.

> What happens when people no longer have to move?

## Catalyst

The turning point came through agriculture.

Reliable food supplies allowed populations to grow. Villages expanded
into towns, and towns eventually became cities.

Surplus meant not everyone needed to farm. Some became rulers,
merchants, artisans, priests, and scribes.

The agricultural revolution provided a necessary but not sufficient condition for the rise of civilization. Population growth, technological innovation, environmental changes, and evolving social needs together drove the formation of early cities. Different regions followed distinct paths toward urbanization, reflecting their unique ecological and cultural contexts.

## Consequences

-   Cities became centers of trade and administration.
-   Governments developed to coordinate larger populations.
-   Writing preserved knowledge across generations.
-   Social hierarchies expanded.
-   Large-scale cooperation became possible.

## Legacy

Ancient civilizations shaped many institutions we still rely on today:

-   Governments
-   Laws and administration
-   Writing and record keeping
-   Division of labor
-   Cities

### Unique Contributions of Major Civilizations

-   **Mesopotamia:** Created the Code of Hammurabi, one of the earliest written legal codes, and developed cuneiform script for record-keeping and literature.
-   **Ancient Egypt:** Built monumental pyramids and developed hieroglyphic writing for religious and administrative purposes.
-   **Indus Valley:** Pioneered advanced urban planning with sophisticated drainage systems and grid-layout cities like Mohenjo-daro.
-   **China:** Developed oracle bone script for divination and produced exquisite bronze vessels for ritual and political purposes.

## Why This Era Matters

Ancient Civilizations marked humanity's first major experiment in living
together at scale.

The modern world began when humans learned not only how to survive
together---but how to organize together.
