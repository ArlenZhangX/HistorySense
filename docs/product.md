# Product Requirement Document (PRD)

## Project Name

**History Sense**

副标题：

> Learn 5,000 years of world history in 2 hours.

***

# 1. Vision

Build an extremely minimalist history learning website.

The goal is **not** to help users memorize dates or historical figures.

The goal is to help users develop a **"sense of history"**:

- Understand the major stages of world history.
- Know the rough chronological order of events.
- Understand how today's world came to be.
- Recognize several major forces driving historical change.
- Be able to explain modern society using historical frameworks.

A user should be able to complete the entire learning journey within **2–3 hours**.

***

# 2. Target Audience

Primary audience:

- Adults with little historical knowledge.
- Students seeking a big-picture understanding.
- Readers of books like *Sapiens* who want a structured framework.
- Curious learners who dislike traditional textbook learning.

User characteristics:

- Short attention span.
- Prefer visual structures over long articles.
- Want understanding rather than memorization.
- Enjoy learning through small, digestible units.

***

# 3. Design Principles

The product should always follow these principles.

## 3.1 Minimalism

Avoid:

- Long articles
- Information overload
- Excessive navigation
- Academic writing style

Prefer:

- One-sentence summaries
- Small learning units
- Progressive disclosure

***

## 3.2 Framework First

Teach frameworks before details.

Users should first understand:

> "What happened?"

before learning:

> "Who exactly did it?"

***

## 3.3 Cause and Effect

History should be presented as:

> Cause → Change → Result → Legacy

instead of isolated events.

***

## 3.4 Present Relevance

Every historical topic must answer:

> "Why does this still matter today?"

History should help users understand the present, not merely observe the past.

***

# 4. Learning Objectives

After completing the experience, users should be able to achieve three outcomes.

***

## 4.3 Modern Understanding

Users should understand:

- Why Europe rose to global prominence.
- Why nation states became dominant.
- Why globalization emerged.
- Why the United States became a superpower.
- Why AI may represent another historical turning point.

***

# 5. Information Architecture

The website contains only four sections.

The entire experience should feel lightweight and intuitive.

***

## 5.1 Landing Page

### Purpose

Encourage users to begin.

### Content

Headline:

> Understand 5,000 years of history in 2 hours.

Subheadline:

> Not dates. Not memorization. Just the story of how our world came to be.

Primary action:

> Start Learning

No additional sections are required.

No feature lists.

No testimonials.

No distractions.

***

## 5.2 Timeline Mode

This is the default learning experience.

History is displayed as a sequence of eras.

Each era is represented by a single card.

### Era Card Structure

Fields:

- Era name
- Date range
- One-sentence summary
- Three key ideas
- Three keywords
- One reflection question

***

### Example

#### Industrial Revolution

1760–1914

One-sentence summary:

> The first time machines replaced human muscle, reshaping global wealth and power.

Key ideas:

- Steam power transformed production.
- Factory systems emerged.
- Europe expanded globally.

Keywords:

- Steam Engine
- Capitalism
- Colonialism

Reflection:

> How would your life differ without industrialization?

Estimated completion time:

30–60 seconds.

***

## 5.3 Deep Dive Mode

Users can open any era for additional context.

Each topic follows the same structure.

### Background

What conditions existed before this period?

***

### Catalyst

What changed?

What triggered transformation?

***

### Consequences

What happened afterward?

***

### Legacy

How does this still affect us today?

***

Length:

3–5 minutes maximum.

The goal is explanation, not completeness.

***

## 5.4 Framework Mode

Available views:

# 6. Learning Reinforcement

The product avoids traditional examinations.

The objective is reflection and recall.

***

## 6.1 One-Sentence Recall

At the end of each era, prompt users:

> Summarize this era in one sentence.

This exercise is optional.

No grading is required.

***

## 6.2 Sequence Challenge

Occasionally invite users to arrange events in order.

Example:

Arrange the following:

- Industrial Revolution
- Cold War
- Age of Exploration
- First World War

Purpose:

Strengthen chronological intuition.

No scores or rankings.

***

# 7. Content Model

## Era

Each era contains:

- Identifier
- Title
- Date range
- Summary
- Key ideas
- Keywords
- Reflection question
- Deep dive content
- Related frameworks
- Estimated completion time
- Previous era
- Next era

***

## Deep Dive

Each deep dive contains:

- Background
- Catalyst
- Consequences
- Legacy

***

## Framework

Each framework contains:

- Framework type
- Title
- Sequence of developments
- Brief explanation

***

# 8. Initial Content Scope

The initial version contains only eleven eras.

1. Ancient Civilizations
2. Axial Age
3. Classical Empires
4. Middle Ages
5. Age of Exploration
6. Enlightenment & Revolutions
7. Industrial Revolution
8. World Wars
9. Cold War
10. Globalization
11. AI Era

No additional content should be added during the first release.

Completeness is less important than clarity.

***

# 9. Non-Goals

The product should not become:

- Wikipedia
- A history encyclopedia
- An academic reference
- A test preparation tool
- A gamified learning platform
- A social network

Avoid introducing:

- Excessive detail
- Complex navigation
- Achievement systems
- Discussion forums
- Endless content expansion
- Features that distract from learning

***

# 10. Success Criteria

The product succeeds if users can:

- Explain the major stages of world history.
- Place important periods in roughly correct order.
- Interpret history through technology, power, economy, and ideas.
- Connect historical developments to today's world.
- Feel that history is understandable rather than overwhelming.

If users finish the experience thinking:

> "I finally understand how the world became what it is."

Then the product has achieved its purpose.
